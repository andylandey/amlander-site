Boiler Questions

How often should I service my boiler?

Your boiler should be serviced annually by a Gas Safe registered engineer. This keeps it running safely and efficiently, maintains your manufacturer warranty, and is a legal requirement for landlords.

How do I know if my boiler needs replacing?

Signs your boiler may need replacing include: it is over 15 years old, it breaks down frequently, your energy bills have increased significantly, it takes a long time to heat up, or it is making unusual noises. Andy can assess your boiler and give you an honest recommendation — he will never push you toward a replacement if a repair is the right answer.

What boiler brands do you install and service?

Andy is a Worcester Bosch Accredited Installer, Vaillant Advance Approved and ATAG Approved Partner. He also services and installs Baxi, Potterton, Ideal, Viessmann, Glow-worm and all other major makes and models. As an ex Worcester Bosch and British Gas engineer, no boiler is too complex.

As a Worcester Bosch Accredited Installer, Andy can offer extended warranties of up to 12 years on qualifying new Worcester Bosch boiler installations. Vaillant and ATAG also offer extended warranties for installations by approved engineers. Andy will confirm the warranty available for any boiler he recommends.

Gas Safety Questions

What is a CP12 gas safety certificate?

A CP12 is a Landlord Gas Safety Record issued after an annual gas safety inspection. Landlords in the UK are legally required to have one carried out every 12 months by a Gas Safe registered engineer and to provide a copy to tenants within 28 days of the inspection.

What does Gas Safe registered mean?

Gas Safe registered means an engineer is legally qualified and certified to work on gas appliances in the UK. It replaced CORGI registration in 2009. It is illegal for an unregistered engineer to carry out gas work. You can verify Andy's registration on the Gas Safe Register website — his number is 199048.

Do homeowners need a gas safety certificate?

Gas safety certificates are a legal requirement for landlords, not homeowners. However, homeowners can request a gas safety inspection for their own peace of mind — particularly useful when buying or selling a property.

What is carbon monoxide and why is it dangerous?

Carbon monoxide (CO) is a colourless, odourless gas produced by incomplete combustion. It is extremely dangerous — potentially fatal — and undetectable without a CO alarm. A faulty boiler or gas appliance can produce dangerous levels of CO. Andy checks for CO risk as part of every gas safety inspection and boiler service. Every home with gas appliances should have a working CO alarm.
