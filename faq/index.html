Boiler Questions

How often should I service my boiler?

Your boiler should be serviced annually by a Gas Safe registered engineer. This keeps it running safely and efficiently, maintains your manufacturer warranty, and is a legal requirement for landlords.

How do I know if my boiler needs replacing?

Signs include: it is over 15 years old, it breaks down frequently, your energy bills have increased significantly, it takes a long time to heat up, or it is making unusual noises. Andy can assess your boiler and give you an honest recommendation — he will never push you toward a replacement if a repair is the right answer.

Andy is a Worcester Bosch Accredited Installer, Vaillant Advance Approved and ATAG Approved Partner. He also services and installs Baxi, Potterton, Ideal, Viessmann and all other major makes. As an ex Worcester Bosch and British Gas engineer, no boiler is too complex.

Gas Safety Questions

What is a CP12 gas safety certificate?

A CP12 is a Landlord Gas Safety Record issued after an annual gas safety inspection. Landlords are legally required to have one carried out every 12 months by a Gas Safe registered engineer and to provide a copy to tenants within 28 days.

What does Gas Safe registered mean?

Gas Safe registered means an engineer is legally qualified and certified to work on gas appliances in the UK. It is illegal for an unregistered engineer to carry out gas work. You can verify Andy’s registration on the Gas Safe Register website — his number is 199048.

What is carbon monoxide and why is it dangerous?

Carbon monoxide (CO) is a colourless, odourless gas produced by incomplete combustion. It is potentially fatal and undetectable without a CO alarm. Andy checks for CO risk as part of every gas safety inspection and boiler service. Every home with gas appliances should have a working CO alarm.
